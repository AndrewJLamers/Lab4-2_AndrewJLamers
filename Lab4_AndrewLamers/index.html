Vision Statement

"Saving the world, one map at a time"

At first, it was just a joke between myself and a classmate at the University of Minnesota. It didn't take long for me to realize that it was more than a joke, it was how I wanted to approach my career.

That may sound like a lofty goal, but I believe everything starts with a small change that we can build upon.


Whether that is making someone's life easier, developing a more efficient work flow, or taking on a new challenge, our goal here at Minnesconsin Mapping is to take on positive change with relentless forward progress.

- Andrew Lamers




Project Spotlight


My experience with coding and writing script had been tumultuous at best.
It has only been since I started my online Master of Science Program with UW-Madison that I started to feel comfortable within that dynamic.
During my first semester we were exposed to CartoCSS, CSS, Python, and HTML (which was used to develop this webpage).
It is because of this swing in experience and opinion that my first Project Spotlight feature is my 90's RPG interactive world map.
Developed using Mapbox Studio and CartoCSS, this project opened my eyes to the extent scripting can be used as a tool for design.
